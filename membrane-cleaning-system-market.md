<p><ul><li>Flushing</li><li>Backwash</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The membrane cleaning system market comprises various methods, primarily flushing and backwash techniques. Flushing involves sending a cleaning solution or water through the membrane to remove accumulated contaminants, promoting improved permeability and efficiency. In contrast, backwash refers to reversing the flow of the cleaning solution or water through the membrane, dislodging trapped particles and restoring optimal performance. Both methods are essential for maintaining membrane integrity and prolonging system lifespan, catering to diverse applications in water treatment and industrial processes.</p></p>
