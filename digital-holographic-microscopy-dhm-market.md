<p><p>Digital Holographic Microscopy (DHM) is an advanced imaging technique that utilizes digital holography to capture and analyze phase information of transparent specimens without the need for labeling. It primarily encompasses two market types: label-free interferometric techniques, which facilitate non-invasive imaging for biological samples, and quantitative phase imaging, which quantifies the phase shifts of light to provide detailed information about cellular structures and dynamics. Both methods enhance the understanding of biological processes in real-time and contribute to various scientific and medical applications.</p></p>
