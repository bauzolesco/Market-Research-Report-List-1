<p><p>The BGA solder ball market is divided into two main types: lead solder balls and lead-free solder balls. Lead solder balls contain lead, offering superior thermal and electrical conductivity, making them suitable for high-performance applications. However, environmental regulations increasingly restrict their use. In contrast, lead-free solder balls, often made from tin, silver, and copper alloys, are more environmentally friendly and comply with regulations but may present challenges in terms of melting temperature and performance in certain applications.</p></p>
